TSA Employee Assistance Program (EAP)

The Transportation Security Administration (TSA) understands that employees are busy juggling work and family life issues that can sometimes lead to stress. Encounters with difficult experiences relating to the workplace, family members, financial matters, and other concerns can affect your overall quality of life. To address these concerns, TSA has partnered with Federal Occupational Health (FOH) to provide you and your immediate family members with a wide range of Employee Assistance Program (EAP) services at no cost to you.

With just a keystroke or a telephone call, you can access your EAP services on the Internet or via a professionally staffed call center.

EAP services are convenient and confidential. Licensed counselors are available to help with difficult personal situations that include, but are not limited to, stress and workplace issues, alcohol and drug addictions, family and relationships, financial, and legal problems.

The following are services available through the EAP:
EAP Services

Access to licensed counselors who provide in-person, one-on-one counseling.

Service Topics

    • Crisis Management
    • Stress and Workplace Issues
    • Relationship and Family Issues
    • Addictions
    • Legal and Financial Concerns
    • Community Resources and Referrals
    • Clinical Services
    • Let's Talk" Newsletter

EAP Website: FOH4you.com

    • EAP Information & Services
    • Self-Assessment Tools
    • Ask the Expert about Mental Health Issues
    • Mental Health Articles
    • Prevention Information

To make a confidential EAP appointment with a licensed EAP counselor, call 1 (800) 222-0364 or 1 (888) 262-7848 if you are hearing-impaired. You may call to speak with a counselor anytime, all day, all week, and all year. You will be offered assistance or given an appointment to meet with a counselor. Meetings with your counselor are confidential.

You may also access EAP services information via the Internet as follows:

  1. Enter the URL: www.FOH4you.com
  2. New users, click on the "New User" button.
  3. When prompted, enter your EAP toll-free number 800-222-0364.
  4. If you choose to register, follow the on-screen instructions. If you choose to enter the site unregistered, scroll to the bottom of the screen and click on the "Continue Unregistered" button.

LifeCare Program and Services

LifeCare, the life event management referral service designed to help employees and their dependents with work and personal responsibilities. Accessible 24 hours a day, seven days a week via 866-888-9803 or www.lifecare.com (user ID and password is "tsa").

LifeCare Benefits

Employees can contact LifeCare for information and personalized referrals to assist with the following issues:

  • Pregnancy & Adoption: Prenatal care, birthing options, breastfeeding, domestic and international adoption resources, etc.
  • Child Care & Parenting: Care options, centers, in-home care, child safety, parenting resources, etc.
  • Education: Pre-K to college, financial aid, scholarships, special needs programs, etc.
  • Successful AgingSM: Short- and long-term care options, caregiving resources, retirement, etc.
  • Health & Wellness: Exercise programs, weight loss, nutrition, safety, etc.
  • Legal Services: Services beyond free consultations with attorneys. Also offer document preparation services to assist individuals and their older loved ones with the updating and execution of critical legal documents such as wills, and various types of Power of Attorney directives.
  • Financial Services: Beyond your typical telephonic assistance with credit/debt issues, to provide free in-person assistance with certified financial planners for help with investments, savings, college planning, home buying, retirement planning, tax issues, 401(k), identity theft, budget analysis and planning, etc.
  • Disaster Support: Provides information and referrals to help in a disaster, personalized recovery assistance, from researching options, making reservations, scheduling appointments, conducting transactions, and arranging for delivery.
  • Daily Needs: Relocation, pet care, home improvement, travel, time & stress management, etc.
  • Care Connection Program: Helps employees who are facing life events (illness, aging parents, new baby, deployment, disasters, etc). Worklife Specialists facilitate a customized online environment that enables employees to “ask” for help - and family and friends to offer to help - without imposing. These coaching and support tools help employees mobilized their family, friends, and co-workers whenever life gets challenging.
  • Relocation Services: Provides employees with personalized relocation packets that provide detailed information on communities nationwide, including: neighborhood statistics, city facts, schools and houses of worship.

LifeCare Kits

Eligible employees will receive Kits containing free products and information. Kits are available for the following topics: prenatal, child safety, adult care, and college. To request the free LifeCare Kit, call a LifeCare Specialist, day or night, at 866-888-9803 (800-873-1322 TDD/TTY Service). NOTE: In order to receive a kit, you must meet the eligibility requirements for the kit.

TSA’s Integrated Conflict Management System (ICMS)

I’m an Employee: How can I use the structure TSA is building for the ICMS?

In a Model Workplace, everyone has the ability and the responsibility to raise issues, concerns and ideas and to respond to conflict respectfully and constructively. TSA’s Integrated Conflict Management System (ICMS) provides skills, structure, and support so that any kind of issue can be raised with confidence that it will be respectfully heard and responsibly dealt with. The Structure of an ICMS includes all the places and ways you can raise and address issues and concerns.

Read below to learn about the Places and Ways you can raise issues and concerns:
I Can Deal With it on My Own
I Can Get Help
I Can Turn it over to Someone Else to Decide

I Can Deal With it on My Own

Have you’ve faced situations like these?

Difficult conversations:

you’ve had a disagreement with a coworker - it’s no big deal, but the two of you aren’t getting along as well as you used to. You want to patch it up.

you have a special event coming up and need to switch your shift. You’re not sure how to approach your co-worker or your supervisor

you’re a manager struggling with preparing to have a difficult conversation with an employee or a peer

you just don’t feel that anyone is listening to you

Glitches in ‘the system’:

you’ve noticed a problem at a checkpoint and want a confidential way to raise it.

you’ve tried to solve a problem or make a request through the system several times before without getting a response

A key to a successful outcome is to raise an issue or concern early before it becomes more serious. Although most often we like to try to handle these kinds of situations ourselves, sometimes we need: someone to listen; an opportunity to gain a different perspective; to discuss options for resolution; or to be referred to a place or person that can address our concern. In this section we will list some of the places and ways available to you at TSA. You have a number of options: make sure you know what’s available in your office or airport before you decide which option you’ll use.

Places and Ways

A good place to start:

Conversation: A good place to start is by having a conversation using the 4Rs. The 4Rs are based on cooperative problem solving.

Eventually, every TSA employee will receive training in the 4Rs.

The 4 R’s mean speaking and listening respectfully, recognizing differences, sharing responsibility for addressing issues, and collaborating to resolve them. Cooperative problem solving provides the foundation for good relationships, good decisions and good conflict management.

Line Management: Immediate supervisors should be the primary resource for workplace issues and conflicts.

Conflict Coaching - Your airport or office may have Peer Conflict Coaches. Coaches are TSA employees specially trained in coaching and cooperative problem solving. As a collateral duty they are available for listening; referring you to another place if appropriate; helping you develop options; and practicing how to raise or resolve concerns. The coaching process is confidential and neutral - coaches do not serve as advocates for any person or position.

Here are some places to turn to if you choose to handle the issue or concern on your own:

Office of the Ombudsman 1-877-266-2837: You can turn to the Ombudsman when you need a confidential, independent and neutral resource that answers inquiries, facilitates communications, and helps employees identify their options to resolve workplace concerns.

Employee Assistance Program (EAP) 1-800-222-0364: TSA's EAP provides short-term counseling and resource and referral services to employees and their family members. EAP services are convenient and confidential.

Your airport or office may also have:

Concern Forms provide an opportunity to identify an issue or concern for those who do not feel confident or comfortable enough to raise an issue up the chain of command.

Suggestion Programs provide an opportunity for front-line employees to submit suggestions for improving procedures or processes.

I CAN GET HELP

"Looking into it." Your supervisor, the management staff, and the Office of the Ombudsman can sometimes “look into it” for you, get you more information, and explore options to help address your issue or concern.

Line Management should always be the primary resource for workplace issues and conflicts.

Here are some other places that have been created by TSA for employees to turn to for handling issues or concerns with someone else’s assistance:

Office of Civil Rights (OCR) 1-877-EEO-4TSA: For complaints involving allegations of unlawful discrimination and harassment in accordance with federal laws, Executive Orders, and policies, any TSA employee can seek the assistance of an OCR counselor. OCR Counselors can guide you in the Informal EEO process and are the way to access OCR’s mediation program, ARC.

Office of the Ombudsman 1-877-266-2837: The Ombudsman provides a confidential, independent and neutral resource that answers inquiries, facilitates communications, and helps employees identify their options to resolve workplace concerns.

TSA’s Office of Occupational Safety, Health, and Environment (OSHE) 1-571-227-2291: Professional staff members provide technical assistance and guidance to address unsafe and unhealthful working conditions while ensuring an environmentally friendly workplace. Local Safety Action Teams (SATs) serve as a face-to-face forum to raise safety and health issues and concerns, obtain input from team members, and recommend solutions.

The TSA Ethics Office provides advice and training to TSA employees regarding an employee’s personal conduct and potential conflicts of interest, such as the acceptance of gifts, fundraising, misuse of position or government property, but does not investigate allegations of ethics violations. For ethics advice, contact your field counsel or a TSA Headquarters ethics official.

Model Workplace Program Office: You can talk to your supervisor or manager about getting help from a mediator to resolve a conflict that has become difficult to handle without help from someone who is neutral. Mediation is a voluntary, structured process in which a trained neutral person assists facilitates a discussion between two or more parties. The mediator is responsible for the process. The mediator does not decide issues. Mediators may be requested by your management from the Model Workplace Office. If your issue involves a claim of unlawful discrimination or harassment, please refer to the Office of Civil Rights (OCR) listed above.

Other places and ways that may be available at your airport:

Your airport or office might have an Employee Council. You can involve yourself in groups that discuss and make recommendations on organizational issues and concerns at your airport or office. Or just ask that such a group consider your issue.

Your airport or office might have a Safety Action Team (SAT) for making sure there is good two-way communication on health and safety issues.

I CAN TURN IT OVER TO SOMEONE ELSE TO DECIDE

Here are Places where employees can ask a third party to resolve their issue or concern:

Office of Civil Rights (OCR)1-877-EEO-4TSA: For complaints involving allegations of unlawful discrimination and harassment in accordance with federal laws, Executive Orders, and policies, OCR counselors provide fair and impartial processing and resolution of complaints during the informal stage of the EEO process. The Formal EEO Process follows the informal process if a complaint is not resolved during the informal process.

Employees may initiate the Grievance Process if they are dissatisfied with some aspect of their employment at TSA subject to the control of management provided the issue is not excluded from the coverage of that process under the Grievance Policy (See TSA Management Directive No. 1100.77-2.)

Human Resources/HR PeopleLine 1-866-274-6438 is the point of contact for the resolution of HR issues. TSA Aviation Operations employees in the field and non-executive HQ personnel may contact the TSA HR Services PeopleLine for assistance with HR questions and issues.

Internal Affairs 571-227-1800: Any TSA employee can report: incidents involving the alleged commission of a criminal act or conspiracy to commit a criminal act by any TSA employee; mismanagement of TSA resources and programs; any act that effects the implementation of TSA's programs and operations; and incidents that can compromise the integrity of TSA's activities.

Disciplinary Review Board (DRB): This board is an option for TSA screeners who have received serious disciplinary actions. The Board will review employee claims and render a decision based on the facts provided by the employee and management. (See Management Directive No. 1100.77-1 (PDF 267KB) )

Merit Systems Protection Board (MSPB): This is an independent Board to which TSA non-screeners can appeal some actions. The MSPB adjudicates: employee appeals of personnel actions over which the Board has jurisdiction, such as removals, suspensions, furloughs, and demotions; appeals of administrative decisions affecting an individual's rights or benefits under the Civil Service Retirement System or the Federal Employees' Retirement System; employee complaints filed under the Whistleblower Protection Act, the Uniformed Services Employment and Reemployment Rights Act, and the Veterans Employment Opportunities Act; cases brought by the Special Counsel, principally complaints of prohibited personnel practices and Hatch Act violations.

Office of Special Counsel (OSC): The OSC receives, investigates, and prosecutes allegations of prohibited personnel practices with an emphasis on protecting federal government whistleblowers; provides a secure channel through its Disclosure Unit for federal workers to disclose information about various workplace improprieties, including a violation of law, rule or regulation, gross mismanagement and waste of funds, abuse of authority, or a substantial danger to public health or safety; promotes compliance by government employees with legal restrictions on political activity by providing advisory opinions on, and enforcing, the Hatch Act; protects the reemployment rights of federal employee military veterans and reservists under the Uniformed Services Employment and Reemployment Rights Act (USERRA).

Conflict Management

The OLC offers other courses in Conflict Management.

Conflict Management

OLC Component Titles

Analyzing Workplace War Zones
COURSE TSA-TEAM-ANALYZWARZONE-0001
Description: A team would be worthless if everyone agreed on every issue. The purpose of teams is to facilitate discussions and foster creativity. Sometimes the same discussions and creative thoughts that help the team can also bring about conflict. Nobody is perfect and not everyone is going to get along. It's important that teams identify and target any problems before they escalate. At the end of this course you'll have the skills needed to recognize why teams stop working, identify why battles begin, learn how to target team problems, and develop the skills to discuss problems as a team.
Duration=2.5

Communication Skills for Resolving Conflict
COURSE TSA-COMM-RESOLVCONFLICT-0001
Description: Although, through the use of good communication skills, conflict in the workplace can be minimized, it can't be eradicated entirely. On the occasions when disharmony inevitably arises, there is a need for a different set of communication skills--those of negotiation, mediation, or arbitration. This course will give you an understanding of the various causes and outcomes of conflict, together with a practical demonstration of the styles and communication involved in the negotiated resolution of one to one conflict. You will also learn how to act as a third party mediator or arbitrator in situations where the parties concerned seem unable to resolve the dispute unaided. Duration=5.0

Customers, Conflict and Confrontation
COURSE TSA-CS-CUSTCONFLICT-0001
Is there anything that can torpedo your work day more than an arrogant, obnoxious, rude customer? YES! Not knowing how to handle one! It's challenging to stay service-minded when the person you are dealing with is being -difficult.- This course will give you the perspective to effectively cope with customer conflict, and sound methods to deal with all types of potentially confrontational situations. You'll be able to identify elements of emotional response that interfere with good customer service. Staying cool, calm, and customer-connected is a critical learning objective for this course. Develop communication skills that defuse customer complaints. Learn and differentiate the best methods to handle difficult customers in person and over the phone. By the end of this highly interactive course, you'll have the strategies necessary to restore customer confidence and move beyond the thorny issue to an even stronger relationship. Duration=5.5

Experiencing Anger
COURSE TSA-COMM-EXPANGER-0001
Description: Like most people, you have probably gotten angry while at work. A disagreement with a co-worker or manager during a business meeting could make you lose your temper. By taking this course, you will learn why people become angry and recognize how you express your own anger. You will also learn simple techniques to minimize angry emotions, thoughts, and behaviors. Since you may have to deal with other people's anger at work, you will also learn what to do when you encounter angry people. Duration=5.0

Facilitating Challenging Situations
COURSE TSA-MGMT-FACILCHALLENGE-0001
Description: How do you deal with the group that has an exceptionally dominant person who doesn't let anyone else have say, or trivializes the contributions of others? How do you keep a faction from taking over? What is the best means of handling direct challenges to you or the group? What do you do when you know someone is deliberately trying to derail your meeting and/or success? What is the best way to clear the air when there is tension? Welcome to the tough stuff in facilitation. In this course you will learn to recognize and deal with the most difficult aspects of facilitating groups. You will explore typical trouble spots, like dealing with side conversations. Discover ways to maintain and channel enthusiasm with high-participation groups and turn around low-participation situations. Learn techniques to help you handle sabotage and passive/aggressive behavior. Capitalize on confrontation by shifting your analysis from "what's wrong" to assessing -what is really going on.- You will also learn the benefits of conflict and apply tips to transform chaos into creativity. Duration=5.0

Getting Past Clashes: Valuing Team Diversity
COURSE TSA-TEAM-VALUEDIVERSITY-0001
Description: Teams have diverse personalities, skills, and interests that are challenging to put together. You may not get along with every team member, but it's important that you can work together to achieve the same goal. At the end of this course, you'll have the skills needed to work with different personality styles, control your gut reactions to difficult people, and deal with difficult team members. Duration=2.5

Handling Conflict with Others
COURSE TSA-COMM-CONFLICTOTHR-0001
Description: No one can complete a working career without experiencing conflict, so it is essential for you to handle this conflict in the most effective way. To handle conflict well, you need to build up a repertoire of techniques. These techniques require you to behave differently from your natural instincts at times. Therefore, a good starting point is to establish what your instinctive approaches to conflict are, and where these attitudes stem from. This will enable you to decide on a range of different approaches to conflict, based on a spectrum that goes from being only concerned about your own needs, to only being concerned about other people's needs. This spectrum gives rise to five possible strategies for dealing with conflict. Whatever strategy you apply, some core techniques will make you more effective. These involve three main actions: confronting the conflict, communicating with the other party, and determining an acceptable outcome. Sometimes, the conflict in the workplace just surrounds you, even though you are not involved directly. You then have to learn how to avoid taking sides, or becoming a scapegoat. Duration=6.0

Managing Conflict in the Organization
COURSE TSA-COMM-CONFLICTORG-0001
Description: As a manager, you will inevitably have to sort out some of the conflict that occurs in your organization. Sometimes, this will be between individuals, but often it will be between teams, and even departments. The different nature of these conflicts is likely to require different approaches, and there are also likely to be a range of particular demands on you in the way that you manage the conflicts. A way of differentiating and applying these approaches is by adopting a short-term approach against a longer-term strategy. The first is categorized by a -quick and dirty- style of containment and reduction.
The second is characterized by approaches that are concerned with resolving the roots of the conflict by finding structural remedies to prevent the conflict occurring in the future. Another form of a long-term approach is to attempt to prevent conflict from occurring. This is, of course, probably impossible, but this approach is characterized by considering in what ways a manager can act to discourage negative conflict. Duration=6.0

Managing Your Anger
COURSE TSA-COMM-MANAGEANGER-0001
Description: You're angry and you want to let everyone know it. But, is this really a good idea? What will your co-workers or your supervisor think of your outburst? Is there something that you could do to control your anger? This course will help you manage your anger so that you will be less angry and will be able to channel your energy into more constructive activities. You will learn the three stages of managing your anger and techniques that will be useful for preventing your anger from getting out of control. This series provides techniques that a motivated person can use to deal with manageable anger. These techniques do not replace counseling or other professional assistance where such attention is appropriate. Duration=5.5

Perspectives on Conflict
COURSE TSA-COMM-PERSPTVCONFLICT-0001
Description: What is meant by the term -conflict,- and what are the general attitudes towards it in the workplace? In this course, three prevailing attitudes towards conflict are compared. These are that:
--Conflict is always a bad thing
--Conflict is always a good thing
--A certain level of conflict is productive, but too much is undesirable. This last view informs the rest of the course, which goes on to describe how a healthy and productive level of conflict can be encouraged in an organization, promoting a win-win philosophy that improves performance. Of course, conflict cannot always be contained at this productive level, and often spills over into becoming destructive. Therefore, learners will also discover how to recognize the signs and symptoms of destructive conflict, so that they can deal with it in the workplace. Duration=5.5

Team Conflict: The Seeds of Dissent
COURSE TSA-TEAM-CONFLICTDISSENT-0001
Description: Teams are a fact of corporate life. Think about all the team buzzwords. There are cross-functional teams, self-directed teams, union teams, functional teams, total quality teams, and project teams--not to mention the concept of -The Dream Team.- If there's anything that's predictable about these high-powered teams, it's the inevitability of conflict. Many teams fail to recognize that there's power in conflict. Healthy dissent actually breeds team success, and that's the essence of this course. What do you need to know about the roots of conflict and team dynamics to help cultivate a healthy mindset about conflict? At the end of this course, you'll have the skills needed to identify the stages of conflict, the awareness of group dynamics that can cripple team effectiveness, and how to build a positive approach to conflict at the time your team is launched. Duration=3.5

Working with Aggressive People
COURSE TSA-COMM-AGGRESSIVEPLPE-0001
Description: Have you ever had one of those absolutely great days at work? Everything is going the way it should, you're on schedule, and you're getting to meetings on time. Then someone verbally attacks you or metaphorically stabs you in the back. Aggressive people are arguably the most difficult type of people to work with. Hostile-aggressives openly antagonize people; passive-aggressives work behind the scenes with hidden agendas. No matter which type of aggressive behavior you have to deal with, you've got your work cut out for you. That's where this course comes in handy. It details the characteristics of the six most common types of aggressive people and provides effective ways you can cope with their behaviors. Duration=3.0

Working with Arrogant and Duplicitous People
COURSE TSA-COMM-WRKARRGDUPLICIT-0001
Description: In certain settings, jesters and gossips can be quite entertaining. However, at the office or on a regular basis, they are not amusing. In fact, their habits can be quite destructive. If you've had it "up to here" with someone who has a head the size of a compact car, or if you're tired of hearing the latest malicious gossip from someone else, you'll want to take this course. After you've learned how to deal with arrogant and duplicitous people in this course, you'll never have to suffer from enlarged egos or minute self-images again. Duration=2.0
