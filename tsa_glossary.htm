Assault with a weapon Includes:
  • Use of a firearm
  • Use of an edged weapon
  • Use of bombs or incendiary devices
  • Use of weapon of opportunity, such as loose objects or equipment
  • Throwing things at another person
Attack In the context of the path to violence, attack is the step at which the aggressor commits the violent act.
Breach In the context of the path to violence, breach is the step at which the aggressor overcomes any protection around the target in order to launch his or her attack.

Credible Threat
  1. made by an individual who has the apparent capability of inflicting harm; and
  2. are made in a manner that causes people to be aware they are the target of the threat; or
  3. place individuals in reasonable fear for their own safety and/or the safety of others, including their immediate family.

Domestic Partner Aggressor Workplace violence involving a domestic partner aggressor occurs when an employee’s domestic problems spill into the workplace. The aggressor may not work for TSA, but he or she currently has, previously had, or wants a personal relationship with a TSA employee.

Employee Assistance Program (EAP) The Transportation Security Administration (TSA) understands that employees are busy juggling work and family life issues that can sometimes lead to stress. Encounters with difficult experiences relating to the workplace, family members, financial matters, and other concerns can affect your overall quality of life. To address these concerns, TSA has partnered with Federal Occupational Health (FOH) to provide you and your immediate family members with a wide range of Employee Assistance Program (EAP) services at no cost to you.
Employee Aggressor Workplace violence involving an employee aggressor occurs when a present or former TSA employee poses a threat to current TSA employees, customers, or TSA worksites.
Employee Responsibility As a TSA employee, you are responsible for conducting yourself in a professional manner consistent with TSA policies. In addition, you should be aware of irresponsible and inappropriate behaviors in others and report any acts of intimidating or harassing, threatening, and/or violent behaviors to the appropriate TSA personnel.

Ideation In the context of the path to violence, ideation is the step at which the aggressor develops the idea that violence is the only solution to resolving his or her grievance.
Impromptu Violence Impromptu acts of violence occur as a spontaneous, unplanned, usually emotionally driven, violent outburst spawned by the circumstances of the moment.
Inappropriate behaviors Includes:
  • Bullying
  • Suspicious activity
  • Stalking
  • Violent or threatening gestures
  • Research into the personal life of another employee
  • Frightening other employees
Inappropriate statements Includes:
  • Verbal or written threats
  • Verbal abuse
  • Inappropriate references to weapons
  • Inappropriate references to death, suicide, violence, assassinations, or terrorism
  • Obsessive interest in another employee
  • Claims of mental illness prompting problematic behavior
Intended Violence Intended acts of violence occur as a planned premeditated attack on a specific target.

Local Assessment & Response (A&R) Teams A multidisciplinary response team comprised of local representatives from the Office of Law Enforcement/Federal Air Marshal Service (OLE?FAMS), as well as, although not limited to, the Model workplace Program (MWP), and applicable Employee Relations/Human Resources Analysts, the Office of Inspection (OOI), and the Office of Security Operations (OSO). Path to Impromptu Violence Consists of the steps necessary for an individual to take to consummate an unplanned act of violence. The steps are Grievance, Ideation, Breach, and Attack.
Path to Intended Violence Consists of the steps necessary for an individual to take to consummate a planned act of violence. The steps are Grievance, Ideation, Research and Planning, Preparation, Breach, and Attack.
Physical assaults Includes:
  • Inappropriate physical contact with another employee
  • Physical assault
  • Damaging or destroying property
  • Acting out anger
  • Use of fists, feet, or body as a weapon
Physical aggression Includes:
  • Intimidating behavior
  • Brandishing a weapon, whether its real, a toy, or imaginary
  • Aggressive emotions
  • Violence against inanimate objects
  • Throwing things randomly
Preparation In the context of the path to intended violence, preparation is the step at which the aggressor begins assembling and putting together whatever he or she needs to carry out the planned act of violence.
Preventing Workplace Violence Achieved by all employees:
  • Controlling their own behavior
  • Reporting inappropriate behaviors they may witness

Research and Planning In the context of the path to intended violence, research and planning is the step at which the aggressor begins gathering information pertinent to developing a plan for committing a violent act.
Reporting Requirements
  • Who:
    • Who was the aggressor
    • Who or what was targeted
    • Who else was present
  • What:
    • What was said, using exact quotes as much as possible
    • Physical actions that accompanied the inappropriate behavior
  • When:
    • Date and time of the incident
  • Where:
    • Place where the incident occurred
  • Whether the aggressor has acted inappropriately before

TSA Facilities or Operations A building, or part thereof, including grounds and parking lots, utilized or under the control of, assigned to, or leased by or on behalf of TSA and/or its components where TSA’s employees or contractors are present for the purpose of performing their official duties.
TSA Management Directive 1100.73-5, Employee Responsibilities and Conduct Provides TSA policies, procedures, and standards for employee responsibilities and conduct within the Transportation Security Administration (TSA) that conform to generally accepted standards of behavior and ethical conduct for Federal employees. This directive applies to all TSA organizational elements and all TSA employees.
TSA Management Directive 2800.12, Workplace Violence Program Provides TSA policies and procedures for reporting and responding to potential or actual work related violent or threatening situations by establishing roles and responsibilities for local management, and describing available national level resources. This directive applies to contract employees or contract screeners who work with TSA employees or perform work at TSA facilities.
TSA Worksite Managers Management officials (e.g., Federal Security Directors, Field Office Special Agents in Charge, and designated Headquarters managers or supervisors) assigned local program oversight at their respective worksites.

Workplace Violence Violent acts, including physical assaults, threats of assault or oral and/or written remarks or gestures that communicate a direct or indirect threat of physical harm, or which otherwise frighten or cause a reasonable individual concern for his or her personal safety or the safety of others, when directed towards persons at TSA Facilities or Operations, or while on or because of their official duty.
Workplace Violence Prevention (WVP) Coordinators Representatives designated by the responsible TSA worksite manager as the local point of contact for coordination of, reports of, and responses to, actual or threatened incidents of workplace violence. WVP Coordinators also convene and coordinate their designated Local Assessment and Response Teams, when necessary.
