What are the System Specifications for this course?

System Specifications Information

The following minimum system configuration is recommended to run this course in its entirety:


CPU:

1.0 GHz P3 processor or 1.8 GHz P4 processor

RAM:

256-384 MB

Screen:

800 X 600, High Color (16 bits)

OS:

Windows 2000 Professional with either SP2 or SP3 installed
Windows XP Professional with SP1 installed

Browsers:

Optional requirement:
IE 5.5 (XP requires IE 6.0)

Plug-ins:

Acrobat Reader 5.0, Microsoft Media Player 8.0, Shockwave Player 8.5, and Flash Player 7

Other:

14” color display
Sound card
16X or higher CD-ROM drive
System must accept cookies

If your computer system does not meet these requirements, you may experience difficulty hearing the audio components and animations that are presented in this course. Note that all information presented in the audio segments is available in the Closed Captioning (CC) window located on the course’s main navigation panel. Press the CC button to see the captioned text.

What is a “WBT”?

“WBT” stands for web-based training. This course is available on TSA’s Online Learning Center’s (OLC’s) Learning Management System (LMS).  You will navigate through the course using your computer while the LMS records your progress.

back to top

What is a “CBT”?

“CBT” stands for computer based training. This course runs on your computer by way of a CD-ROM. You will navigate through the course menu and controls on your computer, and the program will record your progress.

back to top

Do I need special software to run this course?

You will need Internet Explorer 5.5 (or above) and a standard video player, such as Windows Media Player, to run the course. If you want to view and print the Job Aids throughout the course, you will need Adobe Acrobat Reader. You can get Adobe Acrobat Reader software free at www.adobe.com.

back to top

How long is the course?

The course is approximately 1 hour long, but it is self-paced. You may spend more or less than 1 hour completing the material. Choose the pace that is right for you.

back to top

I don’t have time to finish the course now. Will I lose everything if I exit?

You can enter and exit the course as many times as you want without losing anything. When you log in for the first time, the computer puts a “cookie” on your computer. This means that the program will remember you the next time you log in. The course will automatically start where you last were in the course material. Make sure you use the same computer – if you don’t, there is no “cookie” on the computer and you will have to start over.

My course starts at the beginning every time I log into the computer. Why doesn’t the course recognize me upon re-entry?

It may be that your computer is not set up to receive cookies. Set your Internet browser privacy settings to accept first-party cookies (or “Medium” security).

How do I know how far along I am in the course?

The course is made up of the Quick Tour and four lessons. The Site Map will show you your progress by putting a check mark next to those you have completed.

back to top

How much time do I have to spend in the course?

This is not a timed course. You may review the material as many times as you wish and spend as much time as necessary to review the material. This course requires that you proceed in the order of the lessons.

back to top

I already know this information – do I have to go through the whole course to get credit?

It is recommended that you complete all the lessons, even if you understand most of the material the course covers. It is always a good idea to refresh your memory on key points.

back to top

I didn’t do very well on the exercises. Will I fail the course?

This course is not graded. If you did not do well on the exercises, we suggest that you go back and review the appropriate topics.

back to top

Do I get a certificate for this course?

No. You will not receive a printable certificate from the U.S. Department of Homeland Security’s Transportation Security Administration for completion of the course.

What do I do if I have a question about the material?

Your supervisor has been trained in all aspects of this material and should be able to answer any clarifying questions you have.

I went back into a completed lesson to review something – now I want to get back to the Site Map. How do I do that?

After you have completed a lesson, you can select the Site Map link which will allow you to navigate to another topic within any completed lesson.
